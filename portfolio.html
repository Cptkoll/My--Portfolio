Past Projects

  • Project 1: Disney Ride Concept and Attraction Creation

    Description of Process: In 2024, I used the Blue Sky brainstorming process to develop a new ride concept for Disney, a single sentence summing up the ride experience: Journey with Maui to rescue Moana from the Kakamora. As I continued to brainstorm ideas, other concepts joined in: "Omnimover Ride System", "Use pressurized air for Kakamore darts", "Ride in Maui's fishook" and more. After that, I used Post-its to order the story and help visualize the attraction. When done finilizing the basic plot, I created a storyboard to outline the ride experience from the guest's point of view. Then, I planned the layout of the ride, including the track design and key scenes. After finishing planning my attraction, I created a detailed presentation to showcase my concept to my peers and teachers. I spent months creating a VR simulation of the ride experience, allowing others to immerse themselves in my vision.

    Challenges and Solutions I found: During my project, I faced many different challenges, from facing technical difficulties with the VR, to creative roadblocks in the design process. Some creative difficulties I encountered were mostly mental blocks, which prohibited my ability to create and effectively brainstorm ideas. To overcome these mental challenges, I found it helpful to take breaks, seek feedback from peers, and engage in different creative activities to stimulate and revitalize my brain. As for technical difficulties, I encountered numerous issues regarding the VR. When I started the VR design, I wanted to create my own VR headset, but I quickly realized I lacked the software and resources to do so. This realization forced me to pivot my approach and use existing VR products instead. Then, within the VR, I encountered even more issues when creating the virtual environment. Firstly, I had to learn how to use the VR software and tools used by the program, which took a significant amount of time and effort. Additionally, I had to troubleshoot how to get the attraction to move in the virtual space, and due to spatial sickness, I couldn't stay on for more than 30 minutes at a time. However, through determination and commitment, I pushed through and completed the project.

    Description of the final product: The final product is a fully realized VR simulation of my Disney ride concept, allowing users to experience the attraction from start to finish. The VR simulation includes detailed environments, interactive elements, and a fully immersive experience that captures the essence of my ride concept. The attraction features an advanced ride system, innovative storytelling techniques, and a unique blend of technology and creativity.

  • Project 2: Design and Prototyping of an Animatronic System

    Description of Process: In 2025, I decided to explore and learn more about the animatronics used in Disney attractions. I researched the different types of animatronics, their components, and how they work. I then designed and prototyped my own animatronic: a simple 5 digit animatronic hand made from Lego technic pieces and programmed using Lego Mindstorms EV3 Python. After building the prototype, I tested and refined the system and code to ensure it functioned as intended. Then, after learning more about how Imagineers design and produce the exterior, I created a detailed design plan for the outer shell of my animatronic hand. This plan included sketches, material specifications, and construction techniques to achieve a realistic appearance. I used a combination of 3D printing and traditional sculpting methods to create the final exterior, ensuring it matched the functionality of the internal mechanisms.
