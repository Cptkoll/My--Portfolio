  • Project 2: Disney Ride Concept and Attraction Creation

    Description of Process: In 2024, I used the Blue Sky brainstorming process to develop a new ride concept for Disney, a single sentence summing up the ride experience: Journey with Maui to rescue Moana from the Kakamora. As I continued to brainstorm ideas, other concepts joined in: "Omnimover Ride System", "Use pressurized air for Kakamore darts", "Ride in Maui's fishook" and more. After that, I used Post-its to order the story and help visualize the attraction. When done finalizing the basic plot, I created a storyboard to outline the ride experience from the guest's point of view. Then, I planned the layout of the ride, including the track design and key scenes. After finishing planning my attraction, I created a detailed presentation to showcase my concept to my peers and teachers. I spent months creating a VR simulation of the ride experience, allowing others to immerse themselves in my vision.

    Challenges and Solutions I found: During my project, I faced many different challenges, from facing technical difficulties with the VR, to creative roadblocks in the design process. Some creative difficulties I encountered were mostly mental blocks, which prohibited my ability to create and effectively brainstorm ideas. To overcome these mental challenges, I found it helpful to take breaks, seek feedback from peers, and engage in different creative activities to stimulate and revitalize my brain. As for technical difficulties, I encountered numerous issues regarding the VR. When I started the VR design, I wanted to create my own VR headset, but I quickly realized I lacked the software and resources to do so. This realization forced me to pivot my approach and use existing VR products instead. Then, within the VR, I encountered even more issues when creating the virtual environment. Firstly, I had to learn how to use the VR software and tools used by the program, which took a significant amount of time and effort. Additionally, I had to troubleshoot how to get the attraction to move in the virtual space, and due to spatial sickness, I couldn't stay on for more than 30 minutes at a time. However, through determination and commitment, I pushed through and completed the project.

    Description of the final product: The final product is a fully realized VR simulation of my Disney ride concept, allowing users to experience the attraction from start to finish. The VR simulation includes detailed environments, interactive elements, and a fully immersive experience that captures the essence of my ride concept. The attraction features an advanced ride system, innovative storytelling techniques, and a unique blend of technology and creativity.

  • Project 3: Design and Prototyping of an Animatronic System

    Description of Process: In 2025, I decided to explore and learn more about the animatronics used in Disney attractions. I researched the different types of animatronics, their components, and how they work. I then designed and prototyped my own animatronic: a simple 5 digit animatronic hand made from Lego technic pieces and programmed using Lego Mindstorms EV3 Python. After building the prototype, I tested and refined the system and code to ensure it functioned as intended. Then, after learning more about how Imagineers design and produce the exterior, I attempted to create my own version for my animatronic hand. I used plantinum silicone create the final skin. I attempted to apply silicone directly to the hand to create the shape (because it needed to be a flexible glove specially made to fit the massive hand.) It unfortunately did not work as planned, and I had to rethink my approach, (see more in Challenges and Solutions section.) So I then pivoted to a different method of creating the exterior of the hand. I will admit that this was not the best solution, but due to time constraints, I had to make it work (this was a school project of sorts.) I got some massive O.R. gloves and cut holes in them to make it fit around the complex mechanics of the hand and it actually worked surprisingly well! I also presented a website featuring tons of information regarding real animatronics and my own, formatted in a clear U.I. that offered 4 different lessons about the what are animatronics and the past, present and future as well. I spent a significant amount of time on this project, ensuring accuracy and attention to detail. The "Hand" took over 2 months to complete, while the research and website took an additional 3 months.

    Challenges and Solutions I found: During my project, I faced many different challenges, from facing technical difficulties with the animatronic, to creative roadblocks in the design process. Oddly, the creation of the initial version of the animatronic hand came very easily, because the design all came together in my head. Some problems with the interior of the hand included complications with the mechanics and overall physical appearance and structure. I reworked the design several times, converting a stiff linkage system, to a more flexible one. This new linkage system involved string (fishing twine for strength) to mimic tendons. However, the creation of the exterior of the hand was a different story. I initially attempted to create a silicone glove that would fit over the hand, but it did not work as planned. The idea was that I would drip silicone onto the hand (with a layer of tinfoil and plastic wrap to prevent silicone seeping into the LEGOs), and it would create a layer of silicone that would be flexible and fit over the hand. However, the silicone did not adhere to the plastic wrap and tinfoil, and it did not create a proper glove shape (and seeped into the LEGOs and made the mechanics very sticky). I had to pivot to a different approach, which involved using O.R. gloves and cutting holes in them to fit around the complex mechanics of the hand. This solution worked surprisingly well, but it was not ideal. I also had to create the website as well, which proved to be a significant undertaking. Then I had to code the "Hand" to move. I had to flash a 32 GB micro SD card with the EV3 Micro Python image, then code it to control the hand's movements. Overall, I learned a lot from this project, and I am proud of what I accomplished.

    Description of Final Product: My presentation included 2 factors: the "Hand" and my website. My website included 4 different lessons about animatronics, including the past, present, and future of animatronics. My animatronic hand was a fully functional prototype, capable of performing a variety of movements and gestures.

  • Project I.I.R.A.

    I.I.R.A. (Intelligent Interactive Robotic Assistant) is a project aimed at creating a robotic assistant (in the form of a highly advanced industrial robotic arm) that can interact with users in a natural and intuitive way. The project involves the integration of advanced AI algorithms , natural language processing, and robotics to create a truly intelligent assistant.

    Updates on Process and Completion: This project is in early stages of development. I have designed the basic structure of the robotic arm and have started working on the interior circuitry and programming. I will use a ESP-32 microcontroller conected to a Raspberry Pi 5 to control the arm's movements using micro servos. The arm will be programmed to respond to voice commands and perform a variety of tasks, like answering verbal questions, providing information, and assisting with daily activities. It will be able to monitor my 3D printer and other devices, and will have interchangeable parts to allow for customization, adaptability, future upgrades, and flexibility in completing different tasks. I am currently working on the programming and testing of the arm's movements, as well as the integration of the AI algorithms and natural language processing capabilities. I am also working on the design of the user interface, which will allow users to interact with the assistant in a natural and intuitive way. I am excited to continue working on this project and to see it come to life in the future.
